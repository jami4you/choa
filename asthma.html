What Is Asthma?

Asthma is a chronic lung condition affecting the tubes that carry air in and out of the lungs, called airways. In a child with asthma, these tubes react to certain triggers, causing:

  • The airways to swell
  • Thick, sticky mucus to fill the airways
  • The muscles that wrap around the airways to squeeze tight

Common symptoms of Asthma:

  • Cough with or without phlegm
  • Shortness of breath that worsens with activity
  • Chest pain, congestion, or tightness
  • Wheezing or whistling when exhaling
  • Learn more about Asthma

How To Identify An Asthma Attack

Asthma attacks are caused when the airways in the lungs are bothered by an asthma trigger. Here are signs that your child may be having asthma problems:

  • Very short of breath
  • Continual coughing
  • Skin between ribs pulling inward
  • Bluish color to lips or fingernails
  • Difficulty completing a sentence without taking a breath
  • Learn more about Asthma attacks

Asthma & Allergies

Asthma and allergies are different conditions, but they often go hand-in-hand. The same substances that trigger your child's allergy symptoms can also cause asthma signs and symptoms. Common triggers in Georgia are pollen, dust mites, and pet dander.

Most treatments are designed to treat either asthma or allergies. But a few treatments help with both conditions. Ask your child's doctor for more information.

Asthma Control

Kids with asthma can live active lives. Your child's doctor, school nurse, and family are the keys to controlling asthma symptoms.

  1. Make sure your child users controller medicine correctly every day.
  2. Visit your child's doctor at least 4 times a year for asthma checkups.
  3. Know what makes your child’s asthma worse and how to avoid it.
  4. Quick relief (rescue) medications for use during asthma attacks.
  5. Have an asthma action plan and help your child follow it.
  6. Give your child's school and day care a copy of his asthma action plan and medicine schedule.

Asthma Action Plan

An asthma action plan is created by you and your child’s doctor. It includes:

  • Your child's prescribed controller and quick-relief medications
  • What to do when your child is having an asthma attack
  • How to identify warning signs of an asthma attack
  • Your child's triggers
  • Emergency contact information
  • Guidelines for pre-treatment before activity

What Causes Asthma?

Asthma attacks are caused when the airways in the lungs are bothered by an asthma trigger. Each child's triggers are different.

Ask for a written asthma plan from your child's doctor to learn how to control asthma and avoid triggers.
